Ultra-compact Binaries

Omega Centauri

The largest and brightest globular cluster associated with our galaxy, w Cen, was one of the first globular clusters to be observed with the Chandra X-ray Observatory. Since then, it has been the subject of numerous research papers due to its large and diverse population of stars. It is also so different from other galactic globular clusters that it has been suggested that w Cen is all that remains of a smaller galaxy that was captured by the Milky Way (Noyola et al. 2008). It is a GC that evokes much curiosity from the astronomy community.

The aim of this project is to produce a high-quality, high-detailed list of X-ray-emitting sources contained within w Cen. This sort of census will be very interesting to GC observers and theorists; those who want to discover how the cluster formed and how its stars interact would value studies based on carefully analysed X-ray data. A previous survey of sources in w Cen produced a list of 45-70 cluster members including sources such as cataclysmic variables and a quiescent neutron star (Haggard et al. 2010). Last year a paper was released reporting 59 optical conterparts of which ~40 were thought to belong to the cluster (Cool et al. 2013). Collaborators on this project include Adrienne M. Cool, Daryl Haggard, and Craig Heinke.
